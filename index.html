spatial awareness, as it relates to,
1) a physical environment existing externally
2) a space, potentially observable through all four dimensions, constructed from the individual’s relationship to the external, specifically the imposed structure that defines the parameters of the relationship.

in one instance a structure supports six walls, forming a cube, following a projected path derived from conditions dictated by the structure.
an action is performed in an endless loop.

observations from the exterior proceed as follows:

he has become so preoccupied with the potential symbolism of the black cat that constantly crosses his path that he fails to acknowledge the dog that is nipping at his heels. the wound leaves a small trail of blood, marking an unchanging path that he seems to impulsively follow close to the edge of the compound.

he has been stuck for many months but doesn’t seem to mind. after all. he is never truly alone with his thoughts since he can occasionally allow the dog to tear through his throat. the pain acting as a temporary distraction which allows time to pass rather quickly.

the black cat operates within two spaces,
1) in the moments before he regains consciousness
2) running in front of him only to disappear between the bushes that line the exterior of the cube.
